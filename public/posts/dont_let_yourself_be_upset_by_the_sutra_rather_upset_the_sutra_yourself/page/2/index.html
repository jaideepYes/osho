< Previous | Contents | Next > ‌CHAPTER 23 24 August 1979 pm in Chuang Tzu Auditorium Anand Barbara. Anand means bliss; barbara means a stranger. The world becomes misery if you start thinking that you are part of it; if you become identified with it, then it is going to become a misery. Identification is the root cause of all misery. If you can remember that you are a stranger here, a visitor, a tourist, only for a few days, and the world is not more than a caravanserai – we stay the night and in the morning we go – if you can remain alert of this phenomenon, that we come from somewhere else and one day we have to go back to the original source, then bliss starts growing in you like roses in a rosebush....
